About Me

I'm a physicist with a strong foundation in both theoretical and applied science. I hold a BSc in Physics from the University of Bristol and an MSc in Mathematical and Theoretical Physics from the University of Oxford. My current work focuses on quantum photonic architectures, where I primarily simulate new approaches to quantum information processing using light.

Alongside my research, I’m actively expanding my skill set in software engineering and machine learning, with a particular interest in applications at the intersection of quantum technologies and intelligent systems. I'm passionate about building tools that bridge theory and practice — from simulating quantum systems to developing scalable ML pipelines.

Open Source Projects

BloomCircuit

For the simulation and analysis of various CV quantum circuits and resource state preparation methods. With a focus on the approximate preparation of Gottesman-Kitaev-Preskill (GKP) states, one can model the impact of imperfect sources by including the spectral degrees of freedom, as well as interpret the state's loss tolerance by quantifying the effective squeezing.

Fake News Classifier

A simple machine learning project that classifies news articles or headlines as real or fake using natural language processing and supervised learning.

NOTE: This is not a perfect model, and works best for U.S. politics headlines, as it is trained on such data.
